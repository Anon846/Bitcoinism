Virtual currency mining.

Written on March 13, 2016

With any virtual currency, you can 'mine' it. How bitcoin does it is taking transactions that happened during a block of time, and as Coindesk describes it, makes those transactions into a list, which is called a block. Miners confirm those transactions, and eventually those miners get a reward back. In bitcoins right now it is 25 bitcoin (All info above from http://www.coindesk.com/information/how-bitcoin-mining-works/). There is also things called 'mining pools', in which miners work together to mine, splitting the reward to how much they contributed.

Why I love bitcoin.

Written on March 11, 2016

Bitcoin is amazing. I love probably every aspect of it. I like how you can just get a wallet online, and then get an address, which you can give out to people, so they can send you bitcoin. It is really cool how stores are starting to accept bitcoin. For instance, Treehouse, which is a website that teaches you coding, now accepts bitcoin payments.

There are other reasons why I like bitcoin. When bitcoin was really new, it was really easy to get a lot of bitcoin. Let's say when bitcoin was new, and I had 1,000 bitcoins, and let's also say the bitcoin was $0.01 (USD). Then, that means I would have $10 of bitcoin. But, today at the time of this writing, if I had 1,000 bitcoin, I would have $422,040 of bitcoin. So that means that you can pretty much make money, with the same amount of bitcoin, over a time period.

However, if you went out and bought $20 of bitcoin, but the price of the bitcoin that you bought dropped, then you would be losing money. But if it rises a whole bunch, you would be making money.

What bitcoin really is.

Written on March 10, 2016

Bitcoin is a currency. It is completely virtual, so it isn't like you can hold it in your hand, or stealing it out of somebody's purse. You can buy bitcoin, you can sell bitcoin, you can give bitcoin, or you can recieve bitcoin. Mostly, people want to recieve bitcoin. In order to recieve/buy bitcoin, or even give/sell bitcoin, is that you need a bitcoin wallet.

On your wallet, you get a bitcoin address. If you had a very nice friend that wanted to give you some bitcoin, you could go get a bitcoin wallet online, or download one, and it gives you a wallet address. Then, you'd show your friend your wallet address, and they could send you the bitcoin.

** There are also other virtual currencies like Litecoin, Dogecoin, Anoncoin, Mooncoin, and others.
